Another unaddressed, yet an existing problem within the criminal and forensic field is that there is no specific way or a specific methodology that will modify the most confidential data of clients, criminals etc. These types of sensitive data should be stored under a high security without letting them to be disclosed to a third party, and without a single point of data leakage. The party requesting the information must also be made clear that the disclosure is made solely for the benefit of the proceedings at issue and that the material should not be disclosed to any other person. An undertaking of confidentiality cannot always be easily provided by a third party. In civil cases, however, there is an implicit assurance by the party to whom the papers are served that they will not be used for collateral or subsequent purposes. So always the data should be secured and if someone tends or urge to modify some of the existing data , there should be a particular ,transparent process which will modify the existing data without altering the existing data. In addition to that there should be a particular way that will sequentially modify the existing data and keep track of modified data and documents as history in the records. So, this is yet another un-answered area in the criminal investigation and forensic field. One of the most significant differences is that blockchains are not visible to the public, yet anyone can keep a backup of a blockchain on your device. The blocks of a blockchain are created using encryption, computers, and electricity. The most significant elements of blockchains are that they cannot be altered, are not governed by any single person, and the transactions can be interpreted by anyone. These characteristics are why people think that in a wide variety of applications, the technology has the ability to be used. But the usage of cryptography to encrypt and decrypt data is a tricky concept . Cryptographic keys are specifically used for data encryption so that the personal and sensitive data is not disclosed to a third party during the communication process. So when researching under the afore mentioned concept and ideology there were several research ideas and practically implemented research projects that used the “cryptographic concept in the block chain technology” so that it will securely store the certain details without being disclosed to a third party. The existing Police clearance certificate issuing process usually takes more than 14 days to receive and some applicants didn’t get it within the expected time. This certificate is issued as a physical document delivered by the postal service which can be lost during the processing and delivering period and also takes extra time to receive. Furthermore, This process has also a risk of sensitive data being exposed to unauthorized personals within the delivery period.

If an applicant abroad wants to get this certificate, he or she needs to wait until the document arrives at the high-commission, embassy, or consulate office previously mentioned in the requesting form. Also, these authorities won't take any responsibility for this document that comes in the post and they cannot assure a specific time for this document to receive.

Besides, This police clearance certificate has no mechanism to verify the genuineness of it to third parties. These documents can be forged and presented, so relevant authorities have no procedure or capability to verify the document is issued by the Sri Lanka Police Department. There is a way to verify this document, which is by physically carry the document to the Police clearance certificate division and then, request to verify the document, and those who abroad have to go through various government agencies to verify this document which takes a significant amount of time and money. So existing clearance certificate process has a lack of efficiency, authenticity, and confidentiality that needs to be addressed using modern technologies.

Currently, in conventional REST architecture, criminal documents, police investigation reports, and evidence reports are processed, rendering it susceptible to both insider and outsider attacks such as data tampering. Since these procedures and processes are not well protected and there is no way to verify honesty, openness, and accountability, fraud and corruption can occur. The transfer of confidential files and reports between police stations is a very slow process that loses resources, time and lacks coordination between different stakeholders. As a sample, if an individual makes a new complaint and it takes a few days to obtain it after requesting a copy. There is no proper mechanism that will sum up the aforementioned problems and through the entire research we are going to find a practical, yet the appropriate solution to the above-mentioned contemporary problem.

Implement a way to issue a police clearance certificate as a digital document replacing the current physical document. So clearance certificate department will be able to send this document to the applicants and also to the overseas authorities via email, without sending it as a physical document which takes more time and money. Still, most of the authorities and companies accept official documents as physical documents, hence applicants can offer this document as an official document by printing this is document using recommended way. And also implement a way to check the authenticity and integrity of this document to any public entity without visiting the police clearance certificate division in Colombo. So it will be very helpful to both locals and overseas authorities.

Implement a blockchain network that can replace the existing police information systems and manual document processing systems. Because the data in the police department cannot be hosted on public blockchain networks, this network should be a private, permissioned blockchain which only accessible by authorized personals and officers. Furthermore, this blockchain network should be scalable, so any number of police divisions and sections can connect to this network without affecting the whole network. And also the fault tolerance, which provides the capability to continue operations and transactions properly in an event of a failure. This blockchain network also should be able to provide key security components suchas integrity, transparency, and accountability which do not exist in traditional police systems used in the police department.

Implement two factor authentication mechanisms to prevent unauthorized access to the system and create a way to securely store cryptographic keys for the blockchain users without storing them in the main servers preventing single point of failure. The concept of “two factor authentications” would be a classic solution that will ultimately ease up many of the security wise, technical wise as well as practical wise implementational hardships.
